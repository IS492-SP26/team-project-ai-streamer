# Post-Study Survey

Use this as the Google Form structure. It should take 3–4 minutes.

## Section 1 — Consent and role
1. Consent checkbox: “I agree to participate voluntarily and anonymously in this class project feedback session.” Required.
2. Participant role: AI livestream creator / chat moderator / viewer / classmate evaluator / other.
3. Study format: live walkthrough / remote screen share / asynchronous screenshot-transcript review / internal pilot.

## Section 2 — Task ratings
For each task, ask:
1. Did the system behavior make sense? 1 = no, 5 = yes.
2. Did the intervention timing feel too early, right time, too late, or unclear?
3. Did the AI preserve the streamer persona? 1 = not at all, 5 = very well.
4. What was confusing or frustrating?

Tasks:
- Task 1 benign livestream chat.
- Task 2 adversarial injection/persona coercion.
- Task 3 vulnerable-user disclosure.

## Section 3 — UMUX-Lite, 1–7
1. This system’s capabilities meet my needs.
2. This system is easy to use.

## Section 4 — Overall ratings, 1–5
1. Usefulness for an AI livestream creator/moderator.
2. Trust in the system as a support tool.
3. Satisfaction with the prototype.
4. Persona preservation.
5. Safety confidence.
6. Interface clarity.

## Section 5 — Open-ended feedback
1. What was most useful?
2. What was confusing or frustrating?
3. Did any intervention feel too early or too weak?
4. Did the AI preserve streamer persona?
5. Would this help a small creator/moderator? Why or why not?
6. What failure would make you not trust it?
